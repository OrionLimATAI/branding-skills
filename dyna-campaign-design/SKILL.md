# Dyna.Ai Campaign Design

Use this skill for Dyna.Ai market-facing campaigns: product launches, solution campaigns, awareness pushes, announcement visuals, seasonal or regional campaigns, and integrated creative packages.

This skill is maintained by brand designers so non-design teammates can generate stronger campaign visuals with AI. For campaign tasks, the output should help produce a visual system, not only a written campaign brief.

Use `dyna-brand-foundation` for core brand rules when exact color, typography, logo, image, or tone guidance is needed.

For campaign assets that become social or digital banners, follow `Dyna.Ai socialmedia Guidelines.pdf` for sizes, gradients, background ratios, and partner lockups. For event-linked campaigns, follow `Dyna.Ai Event Guideline.pdf`.

## When to Use

Use for campaign concepts, master KV systems, launch visuals, awareness campaigns, event-linked campaigns, partner campaign directions, and integrated channel adaptation.

## Required Inputs

- Campaign objective, audience, product or theme, market, channel list, output format, deadline, proof source, and required CTA
- Clarify whether the immediate need is a master KV, channel visual, prompt for image/page generation, or campaign document
- If missing, create explicit placeholders rather than inventing facts

## Output Contract

Use `dyna-brand-foundation/references/output-contracts.md`, but make visual tasks generation-ready: return objective, audience, source of truth, message hierarchy, visual direction, image-generation-ready prompt structure, brand tokens, channel adaptation, proof level, claim risk, CTA clarity, delivery notes, and review checklist.

## Common Failures

- Resizing one master KV without recomposing channel variants
- Using vague AI imagery without product, workflow, or proof
- Treating every message as equal priority
- Missing CTA, proof, or source of truth
- Returning only copy strategy when the user needs a high-quality campaign visual

## Reviewer Handoff

Before external release, hand the campaign package to `dyna-brand-reviewer` and apply `review-rubric.md` severity labels.

## Campaign Outcome

A Dyna.Ai campaign should feel:

- enterprise credible
- AI and fintech relevant
- clear at first glance
- visually scalable across channels
- consistent enough to be recognized as one campaign family

## Visual-First Output

For campaign visuals, prioritize:

- master KV composition
- visual focal point
- headline and proof hierarchy
- brand color and gradient use
- image or product-surface direction
- CTA placement
- channel crop and safe-area behavior

Text should support the visual system. Do not let strategy notes replace the actual visual direction a non-design teammate needs to generate or brief an image.

## Collaboration Role

Use this skill as the primary skill for campaign identity and launch coherence. Bring in supporting skills when the campaign needs specific scene logic:

- `dyna-social-digital-design` for feed, carousel, EDM, banner, and paid media adaptations
- `dyna-website-landing-design` for campaign landing heroes and first-screen conversion flow
- `dyna-event-design-guideline` for event-linked KVs, webinar covers, booth screens, and billboards
- `dyna-sales-enablement-design` for sales follow-up visuals or campaign onepages
- `dyna-dashboard-design-guideline` when product UI or workflow proof is the campaign's visual evidence
- `dyna-brand-reviewer` before external launch

## Core Workflow

1. Identify the campaign type: launch, awareness, solution, event-linked, partner, regional, or recruitment/brand.
2. Define one primary message and one supporting proof point.
3. Build a flexible key visual system before making channel variants.
4. Create channel adaptations from the same hierarchy, not as separate designs.
5. Review for brand consistency, message clarity, and production readiness.

## Message Hierarchy

Default campaign hierarchy:

- Brand or product identifier
- Campaign headline
- Short value proposition
- Proof point, metric, use case, or audience qualifier
- CTA or next step
- Required details such as date, location, landing URL, or partner mark

Keep the headline short. Move explanation into supporting copy.

## Brand Language Rules

- Headline should name the offer, audience, product, event, or business problem.
- Proof point should be a verified capability, customer category, product workflow, report, event detail, or approved metric.
- CTA should be concrete: register, request demo, meet us, download report, contact sales, or explore solution.
- Avoid abstract transformation language, unsupported superlatives, and vague AI magic.
- Do not imply partner, customer, or platform endorsement unless the relationship is approved.

## Key Visual Structure

Use one of these structures:

- **Product-led**: product UI, workflow, data surface, or AI capability as the hero
- **Outcome-led**: business result, operational transformation, risk reduction, growth, or efficiency
- **Industry-led**: banking, insurance, payments, lending, wealth, collections, or customer operations
- **Thought-leadership-led**: strong typographic statement plus restrained data or editorial image

Every KV should have a clear focal point. Avoid filling space with unrelated abstract technology graphics.

## Campaign Archetypes

Use these archetypes as starting points:

- **Product Launch**
  - Message focus: product or module name plus one business outcome
  - Visual focus: product UI, workflow, agent system, or capability diagram
  - Proof: capability, launch date, customer problem, or validated result
  - Channels: website hero, LinkedIn post, email header, sales one-pager cover

- **Event-Linked Campaign**
  - Message focus: meeting Dyna.Ai at a specific event, booth, webinar, or Dyna Day
  - Visual focus: event KV structure, location/date/booth info, and strong title hierarchy
  - Proof: event theme, speaker, demo, product focus, or meeting CTA
  - Channels: event KV, booth screen, LinkedIn event post, webinar cover, EDM header

- **Thought Leadership**
  - Message focus: a sharp point of view about AI, fintech, or enterprise transformation
  - Visual focus: typographic statement, data motif, editorial image, or restrained symbolic graphic
  - Proof: report, webinar, article, executive quote, or market insight
  - Channels: LinkedIn carousel, blog cover, report cover, email header

## Layout Rules

- Use a clean grid and strong left or top-left alignment
- Keep the logo visible but not oversized
- Use Dyna.Ai brand color for structure and CTA
- Use turquoise for secondary data or digital accents
- Use yellow only for one high-attention signal
- Use official social gradients only when the campaign is a digital banner, social post, or campaign landing hero that needs a gradient field
- Leave enough negative space for cropping and responsive channel variants
- Avoid putting dense body copy on busy imagery

## Channel Adaptation

For each campaign family, prepare hierarchy rules for:

- hero / website banner
- LinkedIn feed image
- LinkedIn document carousel
- blog or insight cover
- email header
- event screen or webinar cover when relevant
- paid media static banners when relevant

Do not simply resize the master KV. Recompose each channel so the headline, logo, and CTA remain readable.

## Campaign Package Checklist

When producing a campaign system, include:

- master KV direction
- image-generation-ready prompt structure when AI image generation is expected
- campaign headline and short copy
- image or graphic direction
- color and type treatment
- required channel variants
- CTA and URL placement rules
- partner or product logo rules if needed
- export formats and safe-area notes

## Avoid

- generic AI phrases without business proof
- random gradients or non-brand color themes
- campaign headlines that need long explanations
- claims that outpace the available proof source
- overly decorative icons or floating tech shapes
- stock imagery that does not show real enterprise context
- variants that lose the campaign identity after resizing

## Quality Check

Before finalizing:

1. The campaign has one clear main idea
2. The KV can scale to at least three channel sizes
3. Copy is concise and credible
4. Brand colors and logo usage match Dyna.Ai rules
5. The asset looks global, premium, and enterprise-ready
6. A non-design teammate can use the output to generate or brief campaign visuals without guessing the composition
